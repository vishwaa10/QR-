👶 Hearing Pappu's Heartbeat
Step-by-step guide to listening to your baby's heartbeat during pregnancy
01
📅
When Can You First Hear It?
Baby's heartbeat begins around 6 weeks. Using a Doppler, you may hear it from week 10–12. With a stethoscope, it becomes audible around weeks 18–20. The earlier you try, the more patience you'll need.
02
🩺
Use a Fetal Doppler (Home Use)
Apply ultrasound gel to your lower abdomen. Place the probe just above the pubic bone. Move slowly in small circles. A healthy fetal heartbeat is 120–160 bpm — much faster than yours. Always use under doctor guidance.
03
👂
Using a Stethoscope (18–20 Weeks+)
Lie flat on your back in a quiet room. Place the stethoscope on your lower abdomen. Move it gently — the heartbeat sounds like a soft "woosh-woosh." It may take a few minutes to locate. Deep breaths help you stay still.
04
🤲
Find the Right Position
Baby's position changes constantly. Try slightly left or right of your belly button. After 24 weeks, the heartbeat location follows baby's back. Ask your midwife which way baby is facing. Listen after a meal when baby is active.
05
🌙
Best Time to Listen
Try early morning or 1 hour after eating — baby is most active then. A quiet environment helps. Warm hands on the belly beforehand can encourage baby to shift into a better listening position.
06
💝
Make It a Bonding Ritual
Invite your partner to listen together. Talk softly to baby — research shows babies can hear voices from 18 weeks. Place both hands around the stethoscope and take turns. This precious ritual creates lifelong memories.
07
⚠️
When to See Your Doctor
If you can't find the heartbeat at home, don't panic — position and equipment matter. Visit your OB/GYN if you notice reduced fetal movement or if baby's kick count changes significantly. Always trust your instincts.
08
🎵
The Sound of Life
A normal fetal heartbeat at 120–160 bpm sounds like a galloping horse. By the third trimester it may slightly slow. Your doctor uses a Doppler at every prenatal visit — you can ask to hear it each time!
🌿 Safe & Healthy Pregnancy
Everything you need for a beautiful, healthy journey — for Mama & Pappu
🥗 Nutrition for Two
  • Take folic acid (400–800 mcg/day) to prevent neural tube defects
  • Eat iron-rich foods: spinach, lentils, red meat, fortified cereals
  • Calcium from dairy, almonds, and leafy greens for baby's bones
  • Omega-3 fatty acids (salmon, chia seeds) for brain development
  • Stay hydrated — 8–10 glasses of water daily
  • Eat small, frequent meals to manage nausea and blood sugar
  • Avoid raw fish, unpasteurized cheese, and high-mercury fish
🏃‍♀️ Safe Exercise
  • 30 minutes of moderate walking daily is ideal
  • Prenatal yoga helps with flexibility, breathing & stress
  • Swimming is gentle, low-impact, and excellent for all trimesters
  • Avoid lying flat on your back after the first trimester
  • Stop if you feel pain, dizziness, or shortness of breath
  • Kegel exercises strengthen pelvic floor for labor and recovery
  • Always consult your OB before starting any new exercise
😴 Rest & Sleep
  • Sleep on your left side to improve blood flow to baby
  • Use a pregnancy pillow for support between knees and belly
  • Aim for 8–9 hours of sleep per night in the third trimester
  • Short naps (20–30 min) help with first trimester fatigue
  • Elevate your feet slightly to reduce ankle swelling
  • Avoid screens 1 hour before bedtime for better sleep quality
🩺 Prenatal Care
  • Attend all scheduled prenatal appointments
  • First trimester: monthly visits; third trimester: weekly
  • Take prenatal vitamins daily as prescribed
  • Complete all recommended blood tests and ultrasounds
  • Glucose screening between weeks 24–28 for gestational diabetes
  • Monitor blood pressure regularly — watch for preeclampsia signs
  • Track baby's kick counts after 28 weeks
🧠 Mental Wellness
  • Talk openly to your partner about feelings and fears
  • Join a prenatal class or mom support group
  • Practice deep breathing: 4 counts in, 4 hold, 6 out
  • Journal your pregnancy journey — it creates beautiful memories
  • Limit stressful news and social media; protect your peace
  • Seek help immediately if you feel persistently sad or anxious
  • Remember: it's okay not to feel okay — you're doing amazingly
⚠️ What to Avoid
  • No alcohol — there is no safe amount during pregnancy
  • No smoking, and avoid secondhand smoke environments
  • Limit caffeine to 200mg/day (about 1 cup of coffee)
  • Avoid hot tubs, saunas, and overheating
  • No contact sports or activities with fall risk
  • Avoid harsh chemical cleaners — use natural alternatives
  • Don't take any medications without OB approval
⏰ Daily Routine to Follow
🌅 Morning (6–9 AM)
  • Gentle stretches in bed before rising
  • Glass of warm water with lemon
  • Prenatal vitamins with breakfast
  • Light walk (15–20 minutes)
  • 5 minutes of deep breathing
☀️ Midday (12–2 PM)
  • Nutritious lunch with protein & veggies
  • Short rest or 20-min nap if needed
  • Count baby kicks (10 kicks in 2 hrs)
  • Stay hydrated — sip water constantly
  • Light activity or prenatal yoga
🌇 Evening (5–8 PM)
  • Balanced dinner — avoid heavy meals
  • Walk with your partner (bonding time!)
  • Elevate feet to reduce swelling
  • Listen to soft music with baby
  • Talk or read to your bump 💕
🌙 Night (9–10 PM)
  • Warm (not hot) shower or bath
  • Gentle belly massage with oil
  • Write in your pregnancy journal
  • Screen-free wind-down routine
  • Left-side sleeping position 💤
📋 Weekly Habits
  • Prenatal yoga class (2–3x/week)
  • Swimming or water aerobics
  • Call your doctor with any concerns
  • Meal prep healthy snacks ahead
  • Practice labor breathing techniques
💊 Supplements
  • Folic acid (until week 12 at least)
  • Prenatal multivitamin daily
  • Iron supplement if prescribed
  • Vitamin D (1000 IU, ask doctor)
  • Omega-3 / DHA supplement
