✦ Just For You, Mama ✦
How to Hear
Pappu's Heartbeat
Simple steps — because this moment is too precious to miss 🌙
✦ ✦ ✦
01
📅
When Can You Hear It?
Pappu's heart starts beating around 6 weeks! At the doctor with a Doppler machine, you can hear it from week 10 or 12. At home with a stethoscope, you'll need to wait until around week 18–20. Don't worry if you can't hear it early — Pappu is in there! 💞
02
🩺
Try a Fetal Doppler
Put a little ultrasound gel on your lower belly. Place the Doppler just above your pubic bone and move it slowly. Pappu's heartbeat will sound super fast — around 120 to 160 beats per minute. That's a healthy little heart beating just for you! ❤️
03
👂
Use a Stethoscope
Lie down quietly, put the stethoscope on your lower belly, and listen carefully. It sounds like a soft "whoosh whoosh." Move it around slowly — it might take a few minutes to find. Take deep breaths and be patient, Mama. 🌸
04
🤲
Where to Place It?
Pappu moves around a lot, so the position keeps changing! Try a little to the left or right of your belly button. After 24 weeks, try listening where Pappu's back is facing. Your doctor can tell you which way baby is lying. Listen right after you eat — Pappu is more active then! 😄
05
🌙
Best Time to Try
Early morning or about an hour after a meal is the best time — Pappu is usually more awake and wiggly then! Make sure the room is quiet. Put warm hands on your belly first to wake Pappu up gently. 🌟
06
💞
Make It a Special Moment
Call your partner and listen together — this is HIS heartbeat too! Talk softly to Pappu, because from 18 weeks onwards, your baby can hear your voice. This little ritual of listening together will become one of your most beautiful memories. 🥹
07
💛
Don't Panic If You Can't Find It
Some days you just can't find it at home — and that's completely normal! Baby's position, your body type, and the equipment all make a difference. If you feel less movement than usual or something feels off, just call your doctor. Always trust your mama instincts. 🌷
08
🎵
What Does It Sound Like?
Pappu's heartbeat sounds like a tiny galloping horse — fast, strong, and full of life! It will be the most beautiful sound you've ever heard. Every time you go to the doctor, ask them to let you listen a little longer. You deserve that moment. 🌙
✦ Take Care, Mama ✦
Stay Safe &
Stay Happy
For you and Pappu — simple things that make a big difference 💚
✦ ✦ ✦
🥗 Eat Well, Feel Well
  • Take your folic acid tablet every day — it protects Pappu's brain and spine
  • Eat iron-rich foods like spinach, lentils, eggs, and dates to stay strong
  • Drink milk or eat curd daily — Pappu needs calcium for strong bones
  • Eat small meals every 2–3 hours — it helps with nausea and keeps energy up
  • Drink at least 8–10 glasses of water every day — don't skip this!
  • Avoid raw or undercooked food, outside juices, and very spicy food
  • A handful of nuts and fruits as snacks is your best friend right now 🌰
🚶‍♀️ Keep Moving Gently
  • A 20–30 minute walk every morning is the best exercise you can do
  • Prenatal yoga helps you breathe better, sleep better, and feel calmer
  • Swimming is super safe and feels amazing on the body during pregnancy
  • Don't lift heavy things or do anything that strains your back or belly
  • If you feel dizzy, short of breath, or have any pain — stop and rest immediately
  • Simple leg stretches and ankle rotations while sitting help reduce swelling
  • Always ask your doctor before trying any new exercise 🙏
😴 Rest Is Not Laziness
  • Sleep on your left side — it's the best position for blood flow to Pappu
  • Put a pillow between your knees and under your belly for comfort
  • Try to get 8 to 9 hours of sleep every night — your body is building a human!
  • If you feel tired in the afternoon, a 20 minute nap is perfectly okay
  • Keep your feet slightly raised when sitting to avoid ankle swelling
  • Put your phone away 1 hour before sleeping — your brain needs rest too 🌙
🩺 Doctor Visits Matter
  • Never skip your prenatal checkups — go on time, every time
  • In the first 3 months: visit monthly. Last 3 months: visit weekly
  • Take your prescribed vitamins daily without fail
  • Do all blood tests and scans your doctor recommends — don't delay them
  • Between week 24–28 there's a sugar test — don't skip it
  • After 28 weeks, count Pappu's kicks every day — 10 kicks in 2 hours is normal
  • If anything feels off — call your doctor. Better safe than sorry! 💛
🧠 Your Mind Matters Too
  • Talk to someone you trust when you feel scared or overwhelmed — it's okay
  • Mood swings are normal — be kind to yourself, you're doing so much
  • Take 5 deep breaths whenever you feel anxious: breathe in for 4, out for 6
  • Write down your feelings or talk to Pappu — it genuinely helps your heart
  • Stay away from stressful news and negative people — protect your peace
  • If you feel very sad for more than 2 weeks, please talk to your doctor
  • You are stronger than you think. Pappu is lucky to have you 🌸
🚫 Things to Avoid
  • No alcohol at all — not even a small amount is safe during pregnancy
  • No smoking, and try to stay away from people who smoke around you
  • Limit tea and coffee — not more than 1 cup a day
  • Avoid very hot baths, saunas, or standing in the sun too long
  • Don't take any medicine — even paracetamol — without asking your doctor first
  • Avoid strong chemical sprays, perfumes, or cleaning products
  • No junk food, cold drinks, or outside food from unhygienic places 🙏
Your Daily Routine
Simple habits, every single day 🌟
🌅 Morning
  • Wake up slowly — don't rush out of bed
  • Drink a glass of warm water first thing
  • Have a light breakfast and take your vitamins
  • Go for a gentle 20-minute walk
  • Take 5 deep breaths and smile at Pappu 😊
☀️ Afternoon
  • Eat a proper lunch — don't skip meals
  • Rest for 20–30 minutes after eating
  • Count Pappu's kicks — feel that love
  • Keep sipping water through the day
  • Do light stretches or prenatal yoga
🌇 Evening
  • Have an early, light dinner
  • Take a slow walk with someone you love
  • Keep your feet up to reduce swelling
  • Play soft music — Pappu can hear it!
  • Talk to Pappu — say whatever's in your heart 💞
🌙 Night
  • Take a warm (not hot) shower
  • Massage your belly gently with coconut oil
  • Write one happy thing from your day
  • Keep your phone away before sleeping
  • Sleep on your left side with a pillow 💤
📋 Every Week
  • Attend your doctor's appointment
  • Do 2–3 prenatal yoga sessions
  • Prep healthy home-cooked meals
  • Call someone who makes you feel good
  • Do something just for yourself — you deserve it 🌷
💊 Daily Vitamins
  • Folic acid — every single day
  • Prenatal multivitamin as prescribed
  • Iron tablet if your doctor gave it
  • Vitamin D if recommended
  • Eat 2 dates daily — natural iron boost 🌟
